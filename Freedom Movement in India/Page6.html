India After Independence

The process of merging the 562 Princely States in India, with the Indian Union was planned by the then Home Minister Sardar Vallabhabhai Patel, who was known as the "Iron man of India". In view of local public opinion, the state of Junagad was merged with the Indian Union. On 26th October 1947, Jammu & Kashmir joined the Indian Union. On September 17th, 1948, Hyderabad was merged in to the Indian Union. After 2 years the new Constitution was ready in 1949. It was brought into force 26th January, 1950 & India became a Republic. The center created Andhra Pradesh in 1952. In August 1956, the States Reorganisation Act was passed. 14 States and 6 centrally administered territories came into existence. At present there are 28 states, six centrally administered territories. Delhi is a National Capital Territory. In 1951 the French handed over their Indian territories to the Government of India. Goa was liberated from the Portuguese in 1961 & merged with Indian Union. Millions of people came to India as refugees from Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka & Tibet.
