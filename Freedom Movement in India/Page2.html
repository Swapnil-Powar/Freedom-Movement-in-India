Impact of British Rule in India

The British rule established peace and order in the country to a great extent. It had unprecedented impact on the people. The following are some of the developments impacted on India by the British.

  1. Uniform System of Administration
  2. Economic Impact
  3. Land Revenue
    • Zamindari System
    • The Ryotwari System
    • The Mahalwari System
  4. Industries
  5. Education
  6. Transport And Communication
  7. Growth of Journalism

The rule of Law unified civil service and efficient police created internal stability. The Governer General, Cornawallis introduced Permanent Settlement in Bengal. The Ryotwari System introduced by Hector Munro recognized the rights of the cultivator over his land. According to the Mahalwari System, the Mahaldar and the cultivator were jointly responsible for paying revenue to the government. On the advice of T.B.Macaulay, Governer General William Bentink introduced English Education in India in 1835 A.D. On the advice of Charles Wood in 1854 A.D. the Department of public instruction was created. English served as a link language for the whole country and created a large number of like-minded Indians . Governer General Dalhousie founded the public works department and laid the first railway line between Bombay to Thane in 1853 A.D. After the introduction of English Education, Newspapers in English and in various regional languages came to be published. The newspapers were used to create awareness and foster a sense of nationality among the Indians.
