The First War of Indian Independence 1857 A.D.

Some of the main causes for this are
  1. Political Causes
  2. Economic Causes
  3. Socio Religious Causes
  4. Military Causes
  5. Administrative Causes
  6. Immediate Causes
  7. The Outbreak
These are described briefly below.

The system of Subsidiary Alliance & the Doctrine of Lapse Resulted in the Revolt of 1857 A.D. The industrial revolution in England ruined Indian Industries. Many social and religious reforms caused serious discontent among many Hindu & Muslim Orthodox sections. A rumour spread that the cartridges were smeared with the fat of cows & pigs. This became the immediate cause for the revolt. Mangal Pandey shoot an officer. The revolt spread to Kanpur, Lucknow, Nazirabad, Banaras & Jhansi. Nanasaheb declared himself Peshwa at Kanpur. The revolt spread on a wider scale in Avadh. Protesting against the policy of the "Doctrine of Lapse", Lakshmibai, the Queen of Jhansi revolted. Lack of unity among the Indians, mutual hatred & suspicion are some of the reasons for the failure of the revolt. The rule of the East India Company came to an end in 1858. Queen Victoria made a declaration & promised to grant the stable Government to India.
