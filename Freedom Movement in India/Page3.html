Constitutional Development in India During British Rule

The main Acts are

  1. The Regulating Act (1773 A.D.)
  2. The Pitt's India Act (1784 A.D.)
  3. The Charter Act (1813 A.D. , 1833 A.D. & 1853 A.D.)
  4. The Government of India Act (1858 A.D.)
  5. The Indian Counsils Act (Minto - Morley Reforms) (1909 A.D.)
  6. The Government of India Act (1919 A.D. , 1935 A.D.)
  7. The Indian Independence Act (1947 A.D.)

The Regulating Act Passed by the British Parliament in 1773 ended the Dual Government established by Robert Clive. The East India Company was subjected to the rules laid down by the British Government through the Pitt's India Act of 1784. The Charter Act of 1813 directed the Government to earnmark rupees 1 lakh per Year for the education of Indians. The Charter Act of 1853 is considered to be a milestone in the history of Constitutional Development. It created an effictive legislative wing. After the 1857 A.D. uprising, India came directly under the rule of the British Crown according to the act of 1858. The Indian Counsils Act of 1909, expanded the legislature & seperate communal electorate was introduced. According to the Government of India Act of 1919, Dyarchy was introduced at the provincial levels. The Government of India Act of 1935, provided basic framework for the Federal Government of India. According to the Indian Independence Act of 1947, India became Independent and two Independent states - India & Pakistan were Created.
